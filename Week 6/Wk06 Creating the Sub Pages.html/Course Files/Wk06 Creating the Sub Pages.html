Remember

You will not create a new.css page for your sub-page.... all of the css  for your sub-pages will be put on the style.css where you have all of your .css styles for the home page. 

Add any new styles for your sub-pages to the bottom of your style.css.

Add a /*comment*/  at the bottom of style.css  where items for the sub-page one  will that are not already on the style.css already  (of course, the items that are already declared and used on the home page that sub-page one share should not be written again ... example, on the home page you used sec1, sec2, and sec3... on the rivers page, you use the same layout format and also use sec1, sec2, and sec3 so do not put them a second time in the .css for the rivers page, just new items that are created.

On your style.css page, write a comment such as: /*  Items for Rivers page below here */ and put the rivers specific css items there.... skip some lines and put a comment for your other child page.  Setting your page up like this will make it easier to stay organized and to spot errors when things do not work right.

Step 02

You will submit  your home page url in the comment section (since your links work to the child pages, you only need to submit the index.html url)

Always check your pages before you submit  by looking at in your browser to make sure that your links work correctly, that your images all display correctly, that your sub-page one has no errors and has the required images and text and has good visual appeal.

Step 03

After you submit your sub-page one, begin working on the final sub-page.  Use your wireframe for that sub-page as your guide.  Remember to ask for assistance if you have any difficulties with your coding, especially if you are trying a different layout.  I wish there was a non-subjective way to measure this... but there isn't. Generally you only lose points for this with things that are obvious. For example, text that is difficult to read because it uses a color to similar to the background, a navigation bar that is directly against a banner or the text of the page rather than having some space around it, use of a color scheme that obviously indicates no thought was put into the color choices (this doesn't mean that you can't use white - it's more stating that if you just threw colors onto the page for the sake of throwing colors onto the page and it is obvious, you'll lose points), etc.

You are almost finished now......Happy coding!
